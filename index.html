Dawei Xi

Upcoming Assistant Professor, Harvard SEAS–MSME

Electrochemistry · Materials Science · Chemistry · Sustainable Process Engineering

Research vision

Electrons and fields as design variables for selective chemical processes.

Charge-carrier management

Programming H+, OH−, ions, and redox mediators to decouple electrochemical and chemical steps.

Multi-phase interfaces

Using aqueous–nonaqueous boundaries, organic diffusion electrodes, and interfacial PCET to enable otherwise difficult chemistry.

Selective enrichment

Controlling ion binding, transport, and release for rare earths, transition metals, lithium, chiral molecules, and wastewater streams.

Advanced membranes

Building electrically and chemically selective membranes for separations, energy storage, and electrochemical conversion.

Selected achievements

Decoupling pH, phase, and reaction time scales.

Prior work includes pH-decoupled aqueous flow batteries operating above 1.7 V, high-efficiency acid/base electrosynthesis for carbon management, and aqueous–nonaqueous interfacial proton-coupled electron transfer for hydrogen peroxide production.
