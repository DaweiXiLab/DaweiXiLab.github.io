Launching at Harvard SEAS · Materials Science & Mechanical Engineering

Multi-phase electrochemical systems for separations, enrichment and sustainable energy.

The XI Lab designs electrochemical systems that control electrostatic potentials to selectively tune the fluxes of ions and electrons on interfaces for selective separation and enrichment of metals and molecules, for energy applications and process engineering.

Dawei Xi

Upcoming Assistant Professor, Harvard SEAS–MSME

Electrochemistry · Materials Science · Chemistry · Sustainability · Process Engineering

Research vision

Electrons and fields as design variables for selective chemical processes.

Multi-phase interfaces

Using aqueous–nonaqueous boundaries, organic diffusion electrodes, and interfacial coupled ion-electron trnasfer (CIET) to enable otherwise difficult chemistry and separation; studying multi-phase flow under electrostatic potentials

Selective enrichment

Controlling ion binding, transport, and release in liquid-liquid and liquid-solid systems for rare earths, transition metals, isotopes, chiral molecules, and wastewater streams.

Advanced membranes

Building electrically and chemically selective membranes for separations; studying electrochemistry in membrane processes and ionics devices.

Selected achievements

Decoupling pH, phase, and reaction time scales.

Prior work includes stable pH-decoupled aqueous flow batteries; high-efficiency acid/base electrosynthesis for carbon management; Faradaic mechanism during chemical reactions and aqueous–nonaqueous interfacial proton-coupled electron transfer for nonaqueous electrochemical synthesis.

>1.7 VOCV in long-term stable pH-decoupled aqueous flow batteries
>90%current efficiency for > 1 M acid/base generation
<100 mVoverpotential for water disociation
>99%selectivity for ion-electron transfer between immiscible phases
