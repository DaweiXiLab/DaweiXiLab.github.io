Research

Multi-phase electrostatic potential control for selective enrichment and sustainable electrochemistry.

We use electrochemical potential, electric fields, membranes, and phase boundaries to control ion binding, reaction kinetics, transport, and selectivity.

Foundation

Charge-carrier management

Our central design philosophy is to manage charge carriers so that electrochemical and chemical steps can be decoupled across pH, phase, and time scales. This strategy has enabled high-voltage aqueous flow batteries, acid/base generation for carbon management, and aqueous–nonaqueous interfacial electrosynthesis.

Thrust 1

AI-assisted electrostatic sorbent discovery

We will develop hydrophobic peptide and peptoid sorbents on porous conductive substrates for potential-programmed capture and release. Automated solid-phase synthesis, high-throughput electro-adsorption, ICP/UV–vis/fluorescence analysis, and learning-guided molecular design will be integrated to accelerate discovery.

  • Targets: rare earth elements, transition metals, lithium, chiral molecules, and ionic contaminants.
  • Mechanism: inner-sphere coupled ion–electron transfer at ligand-decorated conductive interfaces.
  • Goal: low-waste, voltage-programmed enrichment and separation from dilute or complex feeds.
Thrust 2

Electrostatic control across interfaces

We study how electrostatic potential controls adsorption, ion exchange, proton-coupled electron transfer, liquid–liquid extraction, mixing, and electrochemical reaction kinetics. This includes capacitive ion-exchange resins, redox-mediated liquid–liquid extraction, and electrically gated flow and emulsification.

  • Potential-tuned Brønsted acid/base sites for PCET and CO₂ capture chemistry.
  • Field-programmable mixing, elastic turbulence, and membrane-assisted demulsification.
  • Redox-mediated extraction systems for selective enrichment and release.
Thrust 3

Advanced membranes

We design conductive nanoporous membranes and nano-confined ionic polymers with chemically selective channels. Voltage gating, Donnan exclusion, and selective ligands will be combined to regulate transport in separations, flow batteries, hydrogen storage, CO₂ conversion, and related systems.

  • Electronically conductive membranes with 0.5–5 nm pores for voltage-gated transport.
  • Selective chelator-decorated channels for metals and chiral species.
  • Coupling membranes with sorbent beds and extraction modules for process intensification.

Long-term impact

Electrified, compact, low-waste separations and chemical processes.

The long-term goal is a science-to-systems framework for selectivity, transport, and separation in multiphase environments, replacing heat- and solvent-intensive unit operations with electricity-driven capture, release, transport, and conversion.
